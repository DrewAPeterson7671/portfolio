About

How I Got Started

My enthusiasm for coding began in high school. I remember pulling up to a stop light in my car and thinking "If weight sensor > 500lbs, then...". Learning to code changed me. I was always abstracting the systems I saw in the world around me in my mind.

Initially, life took me in different direction from coding. In other careers, I was always tinkering with spreadsheets. I analyzed sales trends and tried to uncover where to focus my efforts for greatest efficiency. I frequently automated complex forms into a spreadsheet form that output the paperwork in PDF format. I automated my tasks at every opportunity.

In 2018, I had a chance meeting with a manager of a coding bootcamp. I had heard of them vaguely, but was unsure about their viability. 45 minutes later, I had done my research and had a mission. I could have this. I could change course, but many sacrifices would have to be made. I began to prepare and gather resources to make it so.

In 2020, I attended Epicodus coding bootcamp in Portland, OR. The school required 100% pair programming, with the exception of our weekly code reviews every Friday to test how each student had absorbed the week's material. Working with others enhanced my ability to work with a wide variety of personalities and learn from differing approaches to coding. My first code review, 5 days in, was a nerve-wracking experience. I was so afraid of not finishing in time. I normally don't drink often, but grabbed a beer when I got home and still couldn't sleep that night. What if I didn't did do it correctly? What if I didn't pass? The code reviews got more challenging as class mates dropped out or failed the code reviews. 40% of the students weren't able to complete the program. In the end, graduating from Epicodus was one of my most difficult and proudest achievements.

The journey continues...
